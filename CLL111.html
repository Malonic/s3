üìö Course Contents

  1. Introduction to Chemical Engineering Calculations: Units, dimensions, conversion of units, and process variables (temperature, pressure, composition).
  2. Gases, Vapors, and Liquids: Ideal Gas Law, properties of gas mixtures, vapor pressure, saturation, partial pressure, and humidity concepts.
  3. Fundamentals of Material Balances: Process classification, the general balance equation, and solving material balance problems for single units without chemical reaction.
  4. Stoichiometry and Reactive Systems: Key concepts including limiting and excess reactants, fractional conversion, selectivity, and yield.
  5. Material Balances for Reactive Processes: Applying material balances to processes involving chemical reactions, with a focus on topics like combustion.
  6. Multi-Unit Systems: Solving material balances for complex processes involving multiple interconnected units, including recycle, bypass, and purge streams.
  7. Single-Phase and Multi-Phase Systems: Introduction to phase rule, vapor-liquid equilibria (VLE), Raoult's law, and phase diagrams.
  8. Energy and Energy Balances: Forms of energy (kinetic, potential, internal), the first law of thermodynamics, and applying energy balances to closed and open systems.
  9. Thermodynamic Properties & Steam Tables: Understanding state properties like enthalpy and internal energy, and utilizing steam tables for process calculations.
  10. Energy Balances with Chemical Reactions: Calculating heats of reaction, formation, and combustion, and applying them to solve energy balances for reactive systems.
